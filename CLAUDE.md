# Professional Website — Build Guide

This document defines the goals, standards, and rubric for building a professional website for a **Senior Technical Project Manager / Senior Technical Program Manager / Senior Operations Manager**. All development decisions should be measured against this guide.

---

## Roles This Site Represents

- **Senior Technical Project Manager (TPM)** — Leads complex, technical delivery efforts; bridges engineering and business; owns timelines, risks, and cross-functional execution.
- **Senior Technical Program Manager** — Manages portfolios of interdependent projects; owns roadmaps, alignment, and organizational outcomes at scale.
- **Senior Operations Manager** — Drives operational excellence, process efficiency, team performance, and business continuity across functions.

The website must credibly serve all three role identities simultaneously. It is not a portfolio of artifacts — it is a **professional brand asset** that demonstrates strategic thinking, measurable impact, and leadership at scale.

---

## Core Objectives

1. **Establish credibility** with recruiters, hiring managers, executive stakeholders, and potential collaborators.
2. **Differentiate** by surfacing unique cross-functional strengths: technical depth, operational rigor, and program-level thinking.
3. **Demonstrate impact** through quantified outcomes, not just job descriptions.
4. **Drive action** — prompt visitors to reach out, connect on LinkedIn, or download a resume.

---

## Required Sections

### 1. Hero / Above the Fold
- Full name, professional title (or flexible tagline covering all three roles)
- One-sentence value proposition — who you help and how
- Professional headshot (warm, approachable, not stiff)
- Primary CTA: "Connect on LinkedIn" or "View My Work"
- Keep it immediately scannable — no walls of text

### 2. About
- 2–3 paragraph narrative — career arc, core strengths, what drives you
- Avoid generic buzzwords; use concrete, specific language
- Mention industries, team sizes managed, and scope of programs led
- Include a secondary CTA (download resume or contact)

### 3. Impact / Case Studies (3–5 entries)
Each case study should include:
- **Context:** The situation, organization, or problem
- **Your Role:** Specific ownership, team size, budget/scope
- **Approach:** Methods, frameworks, decisions made
- **Outcome:** Quantified results — % improvement, $ saved, time reduced, teams scaled
- **Strategic tensions navigated:** Tradeoffs, competing priorities, stakeholder complexity
- **Collaboration map:** Key stakeholders involved and alignment achieved

> Example framing: "Led a cross-functional program of 14 teams across 3 time zones to migrate a $4M legacy system, delivering 6 weeks ahead of schedule and 12% under budget."

### 4. Skills & Expertise
Group into logical clusters:
- **Program & Project Delivery:** Agile, Scrum, SAFe, PRINCE2, Waterfall, Risk Management, OKRs
- **Technical Fluency:** Systems architecture awareness, SDLC, DevOps, cloud platforms (AWS/GCP/Azure), data pipelines
- **Operations:** Process optimization, capacity planning, vendor management, SLA/SLO ownership, continuous improvement (Lean/Six Sigma)
- **Tools:** Jira, Confluence, Asana, Smartsheet, Monday.com, Tableau, Salesforce, Slack
- **Leadership:** Stakeholder management, executive reporting, team building, org design, change management

### 5. Credentials & Education
- Certifications: PMP, PgMP, CSM, SAFe, AWS, Lean Six Sigma, ITIL — display prominently with badge icons if available
- Degrees with institution and year
- Keep this section scannable — use a grid or icon-based layout

### 6. Testimonials / Social Proof
- 2–4 short quotes from managers, peers, or stakeholders
- Include name, title, and relationship ("Direct Manager at Acme Corp")
- Focus on impact, leadership, and collaboration — not just personality

### 7. Contact
- Simple form: Name, Email, Message
- Direct email link as a fallback
- LinkedIn profile link
- Optional: Calendly embed for direct scheduling
- No phone number required unless desired

### 8. Optional: Blog / Thought Leadership
- Short articles or perspectives on program management, operations, AI in the workplace, delivery at scale
- Positions you as a domain authority
- 2026 note: Feature AI-assisted thinking to signal "AI-ready" capability — show strategic judgment, not just tool use

## Design Standards

### Visual Tone
- **Professional, clean, and modern** — not flashy or overly creative
- Neutral or muted palette with one strong accent color
- Generous whitespace — avoids visual clutter
- Typography: Sans-serif primary (Inter, Lato, or similar); legible at all sizes

### Layout
- Mobile-first and fully responsive
- Top navigation: 4–5 items max, no clever/vague labels
- Use clear section labels: About, Work, Skills, Contact
- Sticky or accessible header for easy navigation

### Performance
- Page load under 3 seconds
- Optimized images (WebP format preferred)
- No unnecessary animations or autoplay media

### Accessibility
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- Alt text on all images
- Keyboard navigable
- Sufficient color contrast ratios

---

## Content Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Tone | Confident, direct, professional — not boastful or vague |
| Length | Hero: 1 sentence. About: 150–250 words. Case studies: 200–400 words each |
| Metrics | Every case study must include at least 2 quantified outcomes |
| Recency | Feature only work from the past 5–7 years unless exceptional |
| Buzzwords | Avoid: "synergy," "passionate," "results-driven," "detail-oriented" — show, don't tell |
| AI signal | At least one case study or page element that demonstrates fluency with AI tools |

---

## SEO & Discoverability

- Page titles and meta descriptions for every page
- H1/H2/H3 hierarchy with keyword-rich headings (e.g., "Senior Technical Program Manager," "Operations Leadership")
- Short paragraphs and bullet-friendly formatting for scannability
- Schema markup for Person entity (name, job title, employer, social links)
- Connect all social profiles (LinkedIn minimum) to help Google's knowledge graph associate your brand with expertise
- Target long-tail keywords: "technical program manager portfolio," "senior operations manager resume," your name + role

## Rubric: Evaluating the Website

Use this rubric to score and prioritize improvements. Each criterion is scored 1–5.

### A. Strategic Clarity (max 10 pts)
| Criterion | 1–5 |
|---|---|
| Visitor understands your role and value within 10 seconds of landing | /5 |
| Tagline/headline is role-specific and differentiated (not generic) | /5 |

### B. Credibility & Social Proof (max 15 pts)
| Criterion | 1–5 |
|---|---|
| Credentials/certifications are visible and current | /5 |
| Testimonials are present and attribution is specific | /5 |
| Professional headshot is high quality and approachable | /5 |

### C. Impact Demonstration (max 20 pts)
| Criterion | 1–5 |
|---|---|
| Case studies include quantified outcomes (%, $, time) | /5 |
| Case studies articulate your specific role and ownership | /5 |
| Strategic complexity and tradeoffs are visible | /5 |
| Work is recent and relevant to target roles | /5 |

### D. Technical & Design Quality (max 15 pts)
| Criterion | 1–5 |
|---|---|
| Mobile-responsive and fast-loading | /5 |
| Clean, professional visual design with consistent style | /5 |
| Navigation is intuitive with 4–5 clear top-level items | /5 |

### E. Content Quality (max 15 pts)
| Criterion | 1–5 |
|---|---|
| Writing is specific, metric-rich, and jargon-free | /5 |
| Tone is confident and consistent throughout | /5 |
| No buzzwords or vague filler language | /5 |

### F. Conversion & Call to Action (max 10 pts)
| Criterion | 1–5 |
|---|---|
| Clear primary CTA on every major section | /5 |
| Contact mechanisms are easy to find and functional | /5 |

### G. SEO & Discoverability (max 10 pts)
| Criterion | 1–5 |
|---|---|
| Pages have keyword-rich headings and meta descriptions | /5 |
| LinkedIn and social profiles are linked and consistent | /5 |

### H. Thought Leadership (max 5 pts)
| Criterion | 1–5 |

| Blog, articles, or insights section present and substantive | /5 |

### Scoring Summary
| Score | Grade |
|---|---|
| 85–100 | Excellent — publish-ready |
| 70–84 | Good — minor polish needed |
| 55–69 | Fair — meaningful gaps to address |
| Below 55 | Needs significant rework |

---

## What to Avoid

- Listing responsibilities instead of outcomes ("responsible for" vs. "led" / "reduced" / "scaled")
- Generic summaries that could belong to anyone
- Outdated work samples or expired certifications
- Walls of text without visual breaks
- Vague CTAs like "Learn More" — use action-specific language
- Missing mobile optimization
- Cluttered or over-designed layouts that obscure content

---

## Platform Recommendation

For a non-developer building this site:
- **Webflow** — most control, best design output, steeper learning curve
- **Squarespace** — polished templates, fast to launch, good for non-technical users
- **WordPress** — maximum flexibility, requires more maintenance

For a developer-built site, a static site generator (Next.js, Astro) hosted on Vercel is preferred for performance and control.
