SaaS · Technical Program Management

Scaling SaaS Implementation Programs from 1 Monthly Launch to 10–17 Weekly Launches

Vetcove 2024 – Present

Context

Vetcove's implementation team needed to rapidly scale SaaS onboarding and data migration programs across a growing roster of enterprise veterinary clinics. At the outset, launches were manual and fragmented across 6+ tracking systems, with no documented processes or PM infrastructure — limiting throughput to one clinic launch per month across 28+ distinct legacy PIMS systems.

My Role

Lead Technical Program Manager across all SaaS implementations and data migrations. Owned go/no-go decisions, cross-functional coordination across product, engineering, CX, onboarding, an external data conversion partner, and 3 enterprise partner groups — plus full program infrastructure design from the ground up.

Approach

Architected 38+ AI agents and automation workflows using Claude and Google Apps Script, consolidating communication across 12+ operational platforms. Developed standardized implementation frameworks and a 42+ article knowledge base covering 364+ mapped data fields. Built scalable training infrastructure and transformed Engineering partnership from reactive escalation into structured root-cause scoping, offloading 3 recurring processes entirely.

Outcomes

  • 200+ programs delivered across 28+ legacy systems with zero PM-attributed launch failures
  • 71% reduction in SOP manual effort via 38+ AI agents and automation workflows
  • 74% reduction in onboarding and migration issue reports
  • QA consistency improved across 364+ mapped data fields
  • Weekly throughput scaled from 1 monthly to 10–17 weekly launches
  • Onboarding ramp cut from 3 months to 1 month; team scaled from 2 to 17

Strategic Complexity

Navigated ambiguity across vendor exports, admin misconfigurations, and product defects across 3 enterprise clients and 28+ legacy systems. Balanced rapid scaling demands with QA rigor by building AI-augmented workflows that separated operational noise from true engineering defects — enabling the team to grow 8x without proportional headcount.

Federal Research · Multi-Institutional Coordination

Coordinating Federally Funded Research Across 7 Institutions in Yellowstone National Park

U.S. Geological Survey 2019 – 2021

Context

The USGS commissioned federally funded investigations into Yellowstone National Park's hydrothermal systems. The research required coordinating scientists, park employees, and labs across 7 U.S. institutions with distributed timelines, publication requirements, and federal reporting obligations.

My Role

Lead Research Geologist and program coordinator across two USGS divisions (GMEG & CVO). Owned fieldwork logistics, cross-institutional alignment, data management, and scientific publication end-to-end.

Approach

Established multi-institution coordination workflows, developed comprehensive ArcGIS technical documentation, and trained 2 employees to full software proficiency within 3 months. Translated complex scientific findings for both specialist audiences and the general public through 3 online articles.

Outcomes

  • 3 peer-reviewed papers published (Journal of Volcanology & Geothermal Research; Geophysical Research Letters)
  • 3 online databases and 6 conference abstracts published
  • 7 institutions aligned across federal and academic partners
  • Research presented to 25,000+ attendees at international conferences

Strategic Complexity

Managed competing institutional timelines and publication priorities across federal and academic organizations with no common project management infrastructure — building alignment through direct relationship management and clear communication protocols.
