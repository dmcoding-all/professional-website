Impact & Case Studies

SaaS · Product Operations

Scaling a Veterinary Clinic Launch Program from 1 to 17 Launches per Week

Vetcove 2024 – Present

Context

Vetcove's PIMS migration team needed to dramatically increase throughput to support rapid business growth. The launch process was manual, fragmented across 6+ tracking systems, and averaging ~7 hours per batch of 10 clinics — with no documented processes and no PM infrastructure in place.

My Role

Sole Technical PM and data validation owner across all clinic launches. Owned go/no-go decisions, cross-team coordination across Engineering, CX, Onboarding, an external data conversion partner, and 3 corporate partner groups — plus the full PM infrastructure build-out from scratch.

Approach

Designed end-to-end onboarding automation using Google Apps Script, consolidated 6+ tracking systems into a single unified infrastructure, and built a 50+ page PM SOP backed by 90+ instructional documents. Transformed Engineering partnership from reactive escalation into structured root-cause scoping, offloading 3 recurring Engineering processes entirely.

Outcomes

  • 71% reduction in onboarding time (from ~7 hrs to under 2 hrs per 10 clinics)
  • 164+ clinic launches delivered with zero documented PM-attributed failures
  • 800+ QA tickets triaged across 5+ teams; ~67% resolved independently
  • Weekly throughput scaled from 1 to 10–17 launches
  • New PMs contributing independently within ~1 month of start

Strategic Complexity

Navigated ambiguity between vendor exports, admin misconfigurations, and product defects while maintaining alignment across 3 corporate partner groups. Built root-cause frameworks that let engineering focus on true defects rather than operational noise.

  • 85% project award rate across 81 geotechnical and civil engineering projects
  • 100% client retention under direct supervision
  • SharePoint standardized across all 3 SoCal offices

My Role

Lead Research Geologist and program coordinator across two USGS divisions (GMEG & CVO). Owned fieldwork logistics, cross-institutional alignment, data management, and scientific publication end-to-end.

Approach

Established multi-institution coordination workflows, developed comprehensive ArcGIS technical documentation, and trained 2 employees to full software proficiency within 3 months. Translated complex scientific findings for both specialist audiences and the general public through 3 online articles.

Outcomes

  • 3 online databases and 6 conference abstracts published
  • 7 institutions aligned across federal and academic partners

  • 2 employees trained to ArcGIS proficiency within 3 months
